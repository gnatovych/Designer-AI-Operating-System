# Principle — Community Disagreement

## The rule

When sources conflict, show the conflict. Do not synthesize contested answers into a single AI voice.

---

## Why this exists

The shipped default across AI answer products is synthesis. ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, and Perplexity all flatten contested questions into confident single-voice responses. The synthesis reads authoritative regardless of whether the underlying sources agreed.

This is the wrong behavior when the question is subjective or the sources genuinely disagree. Synthesis in that case does three things at once: it averages away the signal that makes the sources valuable, it manufactures false consensus, and it strips the user's ability to evaluate the evidence themselves.

For a product whose differentiation is authentic human discussion, collapsing disagreement into synthesis is self-destruction. The moat is the disagreement. Flattening it into a single AI voice hands the entire category to whoever does synthesis best — which is not going to be the product built on community.

---

## What this requires

**Show the split.** When the community is divided, the divide is the answer. Surface the range of opinion, not a midpoint. Name the disagreement explicitly.

**Attribute.** Synthesis strips authorship. Individual source material preserves it. Users can evaluate a review from someone who lives in the city differently from a review from someone passing through — but only if attribution is structurally present, not buried in a sources drawer.

**Preserve recency.** Opinion changes. A recommendation from 2022 is not equivalent to a recommendation from last month, even on the same topic. The system surfaces when, not just what.

**Decline when signal is absent.** If no reliable community signal exists, decline to recommend. Do not fill the gap with model inference. The absence of signal is itself information.

---

## What this is not

This principle does not apply to factual questions where one answer is correct. "What year did X happen" is not a disagreement surface — it's a lookup. Synthesis is appropriate when the underlying sources agree and the question has a determinate answer.

The principle governs questions where the answer is community-attested opinion, not verifiable fact. Restaurant recommendations, product reviews, experience reports, contested interpretations. Questions where the value of the answer depends on who gave it and when.

---

## Failure mode if violated

The product becomes a source for someone else's answer engine rather than an answer engine leveraging its own source material. Users get synthesized answers and have no reason to prefer this product over ChatGPT, which does synthesis better and at larger scale.

The structural advantage — real people, real disagreement, real recency — is invisible when it's been averaged into a single synthetic voice.

---

## Opposing view (shipped)

- ChatGPT: synthesizes contested answers into confident single responses
- Google AI Overviews: flattens disagreement into summary
- Perplexity: synthesizes across sources, citations as footnotes

All three ship synthesis as the default for contested questions. Disagreement-as-surface is the minority position.
